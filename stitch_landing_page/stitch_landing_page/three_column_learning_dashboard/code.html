Neural Networks & Logic

Current Topic Difficulty: Intermediate

In this comprehensive module, we explore the foundations of Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs). Inspired by the biological structure of the human brain, these mathematical models form the backbone of modern deep learning.

We focus specifically on the role of neurons as computational units. Each neuron receives input, performs a weighted sum, and passes the result through an activation function.

Key concepts covered in this video:

  • 1 The Forward Pass: How data flows from input to output layers.
  • 2 Weight Initialization: Starting the training process on the right foot.
  • 3 Loss Functions: Calculating the distance between prediction and reality.

Did you know?

The "vanishing gradient" problem was one of the primary hurdles that researchers had to solve before deep neural networks could become practical for large-scale tasks.
